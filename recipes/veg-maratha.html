Veg Maratha

Description

Veg Maratha is a flavorful vegetarian dish from Maharashtra, India. It features crispy vegetable koftas (dumplings) bathed in a rich and spicy gravy. The koftas are typically made with a combination of boiled potatoes, grated carrots, chopped cabbage, and fresh herbs, offering a textural contrast to the smooth and creamy gravy. The gravy itself is a vibrant blend of tomatoes, onions, spices like coriander and red chili powder, and sometimes includes a touch of cashew paste or coconut for added richness. Veg Maratha is a perfect main course, often served with rice or roti (flatbread).

Ingredients

For the Kofta

  • Boiled potatoes (mashed)
  • Grated carrots
  • Chopped cabbage
  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Corn flour
  • All-purpose flour (optional)
  • Spices (coriander powder, red chili powder, ginger-garlic paste)
  • Salt
  • Oil (for frying)

For the Gravy

  • Onions (chopped)
  • Tomatoes (chopped or pureed)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Cashew paste (optional)
  • Spices (coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala)
  • Coconut (grated, optional)
  • Oil
  • Water or vegetable broth
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Make the Koftas: Combine mashed potatoes, grated carrots, chopped cabbage, coriander leaves, corn flour, spices, and salt. Mix well and form small balls. Shallow or deep fry the koftas until golden brown.
  2. Prepare the Gravy: Saut√© onions in oil until translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant. Add tomatoes and cook until softened.
  3. Grind cashews (if using) with a little water to make a paste. Add the cashew paste, spices, and coconut (if using) to the pan. Cook for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in water or vegetable broth to create desired gravy consistency. Season with salt.
  5. Gently add the fried koftas to the gravy and simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.
